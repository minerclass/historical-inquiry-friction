The Stance of the Balanced History Teacher

Teaching history under polarization is not about finding a safe middle ground that avoids conflict. It is about **restoring the struggle of the discipline** itself.

Balanced history does not tell students what to think, and it doesn't sanitise difficult events. Instead, it teaches them **how to think** using rigorous, source-grounded methods. When teachers protect this friction, the classroom shifts from a battleground of opinions to a workshop of evidence.

Discipline vs. Opinion

The fastest way to lose a classroom is to allow opinions to stand in place of inquiry. When a teacher enforces civility without requiring evidence, they create a false, superficial consensus. When they allow unevidenced personal attacks, trust dissolves.

By keeping the focus strictly on **evidence, accountability, and empathy**, we build a resilient space where students feel safe to stretch their thinking.
