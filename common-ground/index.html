The staffroom mentor

“You won't avoid the hard moments — and you shouldn't. Lead with your C.O.R.E. and your H.E.A.R.T.: keep it rigorous, keep it humane, and teach them how to think, not what to think.”

You'll face five classroom moments. Each choice moves three things: Civil Discourse, Academic Rigor, and Student Trust. Cheap moves spike one and tank another — the craft is holding all three.

Built on Micah Miner's C.O.R.E. (Critical Thinking, Openness, Respect, Engagement) and H.E.A.R.T. (Honesty, Empathy, Accountability, Responsibility, Thoughtfulness) frameworks and "Teaching Balanced History in a Polarized World." Scenarios are nonpartisan and process-focused.
